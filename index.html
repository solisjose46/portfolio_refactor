About

Background

I was born and raised in Phoenix, Arizona. I enlisted in the US Army immediately after graduating high school. After completing my enlistment, I studied computer science at Foothill Community College where I earned my associate degree. During my last year at Foothill, I also participated in a cybersecurity program where I earned three cybersecurity certificates. Currently, I am a software engineering student at Arizona State University and intern as software engineer at Comtech Telecommunications. Also, I am part of Synack Red Team where I freelance bug bounty.

Programming Journey and Education

My first exposure to programming was in my high school year biology class when creating a presentation using HTML and CSS. However, I was also interested in mathematics and physics, so I started my college education as a mechanical engineering major. Prior to starting college, I enlisted in the US Army. During my final deployment to Afghanistan, I was self-teaching myself Java during my downtime. Once I was in college, along being enrolled in classes that are shared among most STEM majors, such as calculus and physics, I enrolled in an intro computer science course. This introductory course introduced to me basic programming concepts in C++, and it influenced me to switch majors to computer science.

After my first year of college, I was invited to study at Stanford University for the summer. At Stanford, I took a course focusing in client side programming which was really an intense JavaScript course. After my summer at Stanford, I resumed my studies at Foothill College; I completed my associate degree in computer science at Foothill College. All of my computer science coursework was completed in C++. During my final year at Foothill College, I met a Navy veteran who introduced me to SANS Institute's veteran scholarship which allows former servicemembers to earn up to three cybersecuirty certificates. Because of my interest in cybersecuirty and limited knowledge of it, I participated in the program earning three certificates. Earning my final certificate, web application penetration testing, earned me an invitation to join Synack Red Team. Synack Red Team is essentially an invite only bug bounty program. After completing my associates and earning three cybersecuirty certificates, I transferred to the University of California, Santa Cruz to complete my bachelors in computer science. Because of the COVID pandemic, I moved home to Phoenix to study remote; however, I was not satisfied with the program at UCSC. I really enjoyed the courses I was taking such as relational databases but the courses core to the program were too theoretical for my liking. I do not blame the school for this. It is COMPUTER SCIENCE after all, but I did crave something much more practical. My transfer to Arizona State University to their software engineering program was the perfect fit I was looking for. My favorite course at Arizona State University so far has been Operating Systems.

Work Experience

Softpoint

My first internship was at a company located in Scottsdale, Arizona called Softpoint which does business in payment processing mostly for food, hospitality and entertainment businesses. My internship was a three month opportunity with the option to extend, but I decided not to extend and move on.

At Softpoint, I was immediately placed to work on Android development. The majority of my time there was to work on a refactor of their flagship software. I was given the task to comb through their source code and identify code which was deprecated and implement modern solutions. During this process, I learned to be very familiar with reading documentation. For example, one of my earliest tasks was to implement dependency injection via Hilt framework. I spent most of my time reading documentation and completing short codelabs. This enabled me to become very familiar with the framework and complete my task. After, I was able to give a summary to some of the full-time developers on how it works and how to implement it.

I learned alot about Android development in my short time at Softpoint. I learned about design patterns such as MVVM and actually implementing them with modern solutions. I learned to work with SDKs because Softpoint’s software needed to support a range of Android based hardware. I learned to use Git in a commercial environment. I have used Git for personal and school work, but it requires more knowledge to use properly in a collaborative environment. Received hands on experience working with JSON and troubleshooting network requests. This is not an exhaustive list but hopefully it gives an idea on how much I learned in a short amount of time.

Comtech Telecommunications

I started this internship right after completing my internship at Softpoint. For both internships, I worked part-time while going to school full-time. I am currently still working at Comtech located in Tempe, Arizona, so I will update this section at the conclusion of my internship. For now, I will talk about the greatest differences between my internship experiences.

The IT environment is the greatest difference. It is a Microsoft environment. Because of this, I believe it influenced the choice of stack for the project I am currently working on. Prior to this, I have experience with languages and frameworks that are more accessible such as Java, PHP and MySQl. At Comtech, everything I work with is a Microsoft product. From the IDE I develop C# code on to the server that the application I develop for is hosted on. Also, unlike my previous internship, I am expected to support and develop for both client and server. I write cshtml daily. The SQL queries I produce are meant for MSSQL, and I test them in SQL Server Management Studio. I use powershell to run scripts. Oddly enough we aren’t using TypeScript but I believe this will change soon.
